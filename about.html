Why we built this.

Title review hasn't changed in 30 years. An analyst reads a 40-page legal document, writes a memo, and hopes the VP reads it before the pricing meeting. Meanwhile, the encumbrance that actually kills the deal is buried on page 23, described in language that requires a second opinion from an attorney.

We saw this play out dozens of times across active deals. The information existed. It just didn't arrive in a format that supported a fast, confident decision.

LandClarity doesn't replace the attorney. It gives the VP of Land a structured, severity-scored, action-prioritized summary before the go/no-go meeting — not after. The attorney still reviews. But the team isn't flying blind while they wait.
